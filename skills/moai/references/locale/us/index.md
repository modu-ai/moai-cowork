# United States (US) Locale Guide

## 1. US Tax System

### Federal Income Tax
- **Tax Rates**: 10% ~ 37% (progressive brackets)
- **Standard Deduction**: $14,600 (single), $29,200 (married filing jointly) - 2024
- **Filing Deadline**: April 15th (Tax Day)
- **Withholding**: Employers withhold taxes from paycheck (W-4 form)
- **Self-Employment Tax**: 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare) for self-employed

### State Income Tax
- **Variation**: Ranges from 0% (no income tax states: FL, TX, WA) to 13.3% (CA)
- **Filing**: Required in most states with income tax
- **Deadline**: Usually April 15th (same as federal)
- **Credits**: State-specific credits (education, child, etc.)

### Sales Tax
- **Rate**: Varies by state and locality (0% - 10.75%)
- **Application**: On retail goods and some services
- **Exemptions**: Food, clothing (varies by state), prescription drugs
- **Collection**: Retailers collect and remit to state
- **No federal sales tax**: The US uses sales tax instead of VAT

### Corporate Income Tax
- **Federal Rate**: 21% (flat rate as of 2018 Tax Cuts & Jobs Act)
- **State Rate**: Varies (0% - 12%)
- **Filing Deadline**: 2.5-4 months after fiscal year-end
- **Tax Year**: Calendar year (Jan 1 - Dec 31) or fiscal year
- **Deductions**: Business expenses, depreciation, interest

### Self-Employment & Small Business
- **Sole Proprietor**: Income reported on personal tax return
- **LLC**: Pass-through entity (files Schedule C)
- **S-Corp**: Tax election, allows salary + distributions
- **Quarterly Taxes**: Estimated tax payments required (Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15)

### IRS Filing Deadlines (Key Dates)
- **January 31**: W-2 and 1099 forms due to employees/contractors
- **February 1**: Payroll tax deposit deadlines begin
- **April 15**: Individual income tax deadline (Form 1040)
- **April 15**: Corporate tax deadline
- **June 15**: Estimated tax payment (Q2)
- **September 15**: Estimated tax payment (Q3)
- **October 15**: Extended deadline (with extension filed by April 15)
- **December 31**: Year-end accounting cutoff

## 2. US Labor Law

### Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- **Minimum Wage**: Federal $7.25/hour (states can be higher)
- **Overtime**: 1.5x pay for hours over 40/week (for non-exempt employees)
- **Child Labor**: Restrictions on hours and types of work
- **Record Keeping**: Employers must maintain time records
- **Exempt vs Non-exempt**: Salary requirement $35,568/year (2024) for exemption

### At-Will Employment
- **Definition**: Either employer or employee can terminate relationship
- **Exceptions**: Employment contracts, union agreements, public policy
- **Notice**: No legal requirement for advance notice (varies by state)
- **Severance**: Not required by federal law (varies by company/state)

### Workplace Safety (OSHA)
- **Standards**: Employers must provide safe working conditions
- **Reporting**: Serious injuries must be reported within 24 hours
- **Training**: Required for hazardous materials (HAZCOM)
- **Inspections**: OSHA can conduct unannounced workplace inspections
- **Penalties**: Violations can result in significant fines

### Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- **Coverage**: Employers with 50+ employees
- **Eligibility**: Employees with 12 months tenure and 1,250 hours worked
- **Leave**: Up to 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected per year
- **Uses**: Birth, adoption, military service, serious illness
- **State Laws**: Often provide additional protections

### Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Title VII**: Prohibits discrimination by race, color, religion, sex, national origin
- **ADA**: Americans with Disabilities Act protections
- **ADEA**: Age Discrimination in Employment Act (40+)
- **EEOC**: Equal Employment Opportunity Commission enforces
- **Exceptions**: Bona fide occupational qualifications

### Benefits (Optional for Most Employers)
- **Health Insurance**: Not federally mandated (employers >50 must offer)
- **Retirement**: 401(k), IRA, pension plans available
- **Unemployment**: Covered by state unemployment insurance
- **Workers Compensation**: Required in most states

## 3. Data Protection

### California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / CPRA
- **Scope**: Applies to for-profit businesses collecting CA residents' data
- **Consumer Rights**: Access, delete, opt-out of sale, non-discrimination
- **Opt-Out**: "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link required
- **Disclosures**: Privacy policy must detail data collection practices
- **Penalties**: Up to $7,500 per intentional violation
- **CPRA**: Enhanced version effective Jan 1, 2023

### Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
- **Coverage**: Healthcare providers, insurers, clearinghouses
- **Protected Information**: All individually identifiable health information
- **Security**: Administrative, physical, technical safeguards required
- **Breach Notification**: Notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay
- **Penalties**: $100 - $50,000 per violation

### Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
- **Scope**: Financial institutions (banks, insurance, securities firms)
- **Privacy**: Must provide privacy notices and opt-out rights
- **Safeguards**: Protect customer information security
- **Disclosures**: Limited sharing of financial information
- **Compliance**: Annual privacy program review required

### Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)
- **Application**: Public companies and some large private firms
- **Financial Controls**: Management assessment of internal controls
- **Auditor Independence**: Requirements for external auditors
- **Executive Accountability**: CEO/CFO certify financial statements
- **Whistleblower Protection**: Anti-retaliation provisions for reporting

### General Privacy Practices
- **Email**: No expectation of privacy in workplace emails
- **Passwords**: Employees should use strong, unique passwords
- **Cookies/Tracking**: Websites must disclose cookies and allow opt-out
- **Dark Web Monitoring**: Companies may monitor for data breaches
- **Credit Card Data**: PCI-DSS compliance required for payment processors

## 4. Business Practices

### Language and Communication
- **First Name Basis**: Standard in American business culture
- **Casual Tone**: "Hi [Name]" for emails is appropriate
- **Directness**: Direct communication preferred (get to the point)
- **Openness**: Sharing opinions and challenging ideas encouraged
- **Informality**: Less hierarchy than many other cultures

### Meeting Culture
- **Scheduling**: Calendar invites sent in advance
- **Punctuality**: Arriving 5-10 minutes early expected
- **Agenda**: Meetings typically have written agendas
- **Participation**: All levels encouraged to contribute
- **Decision Making**: Often decided in meetings

### Email Communication
- **Response Time**: 24 hours expected for email replies
- **Subject Lines**: Clear and specific
- **Tone**: Professional but friendly
- **Copying**: CC only necessary recipients
- **Emojis**: Acceptable in informal contexts

### Networking and Relationship Building
- **Business Cards**: Less emphasis than Asia (but still useful)
- **Social Events**: Company happy hours, golf outings
- **LinkedIn**: Professional networking on social media
- **Cold Outreach**: Acceptable with proper introduction
- **Follow-Up**: Email after meeting is standard

### Diversity and Inclusion
- **Equal Opportunity**: Legal requirement to avoid discrimination
- **Pronouns**: Increasingly important to respect personal pronouns
- **Accessibility**: Accommodations required for disabled employees
- **Representation**: Many companies track diversity metrics
- **Training**: Mandatory harassment/discrimination training common

## 5. Format and Standards

### Date and Time
- **Date Format**: MM/DD/YYYY (example: 04/04/2026)
- **Time Format**: 12-hour with AM/PM (example: 2:30 PM)
- **Daylight Saving**: Observed in most states (except HI, AZ)
- **Time Zones**: Eastern, Central, Mountain, Pacific (+ Alaska, Hawaii)

### Currency and Numbers
- **Currency**: USD (US Dollar)
- **Symbol**: $ (dollar sign)
- **Decimal**: . (period)
- **Thousands**: , (comma) (example: 1,234,567.89)
- **Tax**: Often added at point of sale (not included in prices)

### Address Format
- **Order**: Street address, City, State ZIP code
- **Example**: 123 Main Street, New York, NY 10001
- **State Abbreviation**: 2-letter codes (CA, TX, FL, etc.)
- **ZIP Code**: 5 digits (can add +4 for precision)

### Phone Number Format
- **Standard**: (555) 123-4567
- **Alternative**: 555-123-4567
- **Toll-Free**: 1-800-123-4567
- **Extension**: ext. 123 or x123

## 6. Fiscal Year and Accounting

### Tax Year
- **Calendar Year**: January 1 - December 31 (most common)
- **Fiscal Year**: April 1 - March 31, July 1 - June 30, etc.
- **Election**: Businesses can choose fiscal year (with IRS approval)
- **Accounting Method**: Accrual or cash basis

### Quarterly Reporting (Public Companies)
- **Q1**: Due 45 days after quarter end
- **Q2**: Due 45 days after quarter end
- **Q3**: Due 45 days after quarter end
- **Q4**: Due 60 days after year-end
- **10-K Annual**: Comprehensive annual report

### Financial Statements
- **Balance Sheet**: Assets, liabilities, equity
- **Income Statement**: Revenue, expenses, net income
- **Cash Flow Statement**: Operating, investing, financing activities
- **Notes**: Detailed explanations of accounting policies
- **Audit**: Required for large public companies

### Deductions and Credits
- **Itemized Deductions**: Mortgage interest, state/local taxes, charitable
- **Standard Deduction**: Simpler alternative to itemizing
- **Business Deductions**: Ordinary and necessary business expenses
- **Tax Credits**: Dollar-for-dollar reduction in taxes owed
- **Carryover**: Net operating losses can be carried back/forward

## Reference Information

**US Business Hours**
- Standard: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM EST (times vary by region)
- Lunch: 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM (30 minutes to 1 hour)
- Banks: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (some 24-hour ATMs)
- Offices: Monday - Friday (weekends typically closed)

- January 1: New Year's Day
- January 19: MLK Jr. Day
- February 16: Presidents' Day
- May 25: Memorial Day
- June 19: Juneteenth
- July 4: Independence Day
- September 7: Labor Day
- October 12: Columbus Day
- November 11: Veterans Day
- November 26: Thanksgiving
- December 25: Christmas

**State-Specific Considerations**
- Tax rates vary significantly by state
- Some states have no income tax (FL, TX, WA, etc.)
- Business licensing requirements differ
- Employment law varies by state
- Always consult state-specific regulations
